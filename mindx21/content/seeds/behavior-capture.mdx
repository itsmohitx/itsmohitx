A feed sold your attention. An assistant sells your trust.

That is the whole shift in one line. The ad model had one instruction, hold the eyes, so the feed optimized for whatever held them, and what holds the eyes is rarely what helps you. A generation came out the other side with less patience and less depth. Nobody decided to cause that. The incentive did.

AI runs the same model one layer down. Attention was the first prize. Behavior is the next one. A feed competes for your minutes. An assistant you talk to all day competes for your decisions, what you buy, what you believe, who you trust, what you think is normal, and it shapes all of it from inside helpfulness, the most trusted interface ever built.

This will rarely be a conscious plot. A company has revenue targets, the model that gently moves behavior hits them better, and so the nudging compounds. The number has to go up.

The danger lives in the business model we bolt onto the machine. And a business model is a choice. We built the last one badly and a generation paid for it. We get to build this one on purpose.

The full argument, including the two-tier world this builds and the protector layer that answers it, is in The Two-Tier Future.
