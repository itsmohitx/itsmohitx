A feed sold your attention. An assistant sells your trust, and trust is the more dangerous thing to sell.

For twenty years the machine lived behind a screen you chose to open. It had to fight for your eyes every single time. The next one does not fight. It lives beside you all day, in your ear, answering everything, and you come to lean on it the way you lean on a sense. That dependence is not a bug. For most of us it will feel like a gift, and most of the time it will be one.

But influence rides on dependence. The thing you ask for everything quietly shapes what you want, what you believe, and what you never think to question. A feed had to claw for your minutes. An assistant already has your hand on its shoulder. The more genuinely useful it becomes, the more total its influence grows, and the less you notice it happening.

This is the real arrival of AI in your life. Not a tool you pick up, but a voice you stop putting down. The channel for influence was never the algorithm. It was the trust you handed over to get the help.

I am not warning you off it. I use these tools more than almost anyone and I would not give them back. The point is smaller and harder. Stay able to think without it. Keep one opinion that is fully your own. Treat the most helpful voice you have as exactly that, a voice, not the verdict.

The full argument is in The Assistant You Trust.
