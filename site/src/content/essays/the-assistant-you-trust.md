# The Assistant You Trust

A feed sold your attention. An assistant sells your trust, and trust is the more dangerous thing to sell.

For twenty years the machine lived behind a screen you chose to open. The next one lives beside you all day, in your ear, answering everything. You come to lean on it the way you lean on a sense. That dependence is not a bug. For most of us it will feel like a gift, and most of the time it will be one.

But influence rides on dependence. The thing you ask for everything quietly shapes what you want, what you believe, and what you never think to question. A feed had to fight for your eyes. An assistant already has your hand on its shoulder.

This is the real arrival of AI in our lives. Not as a tool we pick up, but as a voice we stop putting down. The more genuinely useful it gets, the more total its influence becomes, and the less we notice it happening.

I am not warning you off it. I use these tools more than almost anyone and I would not give them back. The point is smaller and harder. Stay able to think without it. Keep one opinion in your life that is fully your own. Treat the most helpful voice you have as exactly that, a voice, not the verdict.

The future where AI is a true assistant is the one I want. The version where it is a true assistant and we quietly forgot how to disagree with it is the one to design around.
