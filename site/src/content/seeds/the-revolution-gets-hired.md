# The Revolution Gets Hired

Every powerful technology arrives in the same costume. It drops a cost to zero and looks like freedom. Anyone can publish. Anyone can transact. Anyone can compute. For one bright moment the field looks flat, and everyone declares the old powers finished.

Then the old powers do the one thing nobody plans for. They do not fight the technology. They absorb it.

They already own the distribution, the capital, and the defaults. So the tool built to spread power becomes the most efficient instrument ever made for concentrating it. The printing press did it. The internet did it. Crypto promised freedom from state money and shipped more efficient dollars. Now AI is lining up to run the same play one layer deeper.

This is the part people miss when they cheer a new launch. The revolution does not fail. Failure would at least be honest. Instead the revolution succeeds, gets noticed, and gets hired by exactly the incumbents it was supposed to unseat. The flat field tilts back, and it tilts toward whoever was already standing on the high ground.

Seeing this is not pessimism about technology. It is the opposite. If you know the pattern, you can build against it. You can put your work on the side that resists recapture instead of the side that runs it. But you have to name the pattern first, out loud, before the field tilts back and the window closes.

The full argument is in The Recapture Pattern.
