# Lifespan Should Be a Choice, Not a Dice Roll

Death, right now, is random and near. You do not know if you have forty years or four, and you can lose the bet to a cell that mutates or an artery that closes for no reason you chose. We have made peace with this because we had no alternative. But look at what the randomness does to how we live.

When the clock is short and unknowable, every incentive points at the short game. Take the quick win. Optimize the quarter. Spend the planet, spend your health, spend the future, because the future might not be yours to collect. A species that expects to die soon and at random behaves exactly like one playing for the last hand. Most of our worst collective behavior is downstream of a deadline we did not set.

Now change one variable. Give people real control over the clock. Not immortality, just the shift from a dice roll to a choice. Watch what happens to the planning horizon. A person who reasonably expects many healthy decades starts to act like it. They invest in things that pay off slowly. They stop treating the long term as someone else's problem, because the long term is now where they live.

This is why longevity is not vanity and not a rich man's hobby. It is the upstream fix. Control the clock and you change the games people play, from extractive and short to patient and long. The biggest curse was never death itself. It was death arriving randomly, early, and on a schedule no one chose.
