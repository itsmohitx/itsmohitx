Every powerful technology arrives in the same costume. It is sold as freedom. It ends as control.

The printing press. The internet. Crypto. Now AI. Each one promised to hand power to the many. Each one, so far, has handed more to the few.

This is the pattern I cannot stop seeing. I call it recapture. Once you see it, you cannot unsee it, and you start to notice it forming in real time, in the technology being built around you right now.

## How it works

A new technology drops a cost to zero. Anyone can publish, anyone can transact, anyone can compute. For a moment the field looks flat. The early users feel it as liberation, and they are not wrong. For a few years the gift is real and it is shared.

Then the incumbents adapt. They own the distribution, the capital, and the defaults. They do not fight the technology, because fighting it is slow and losing. They absorb it instead. The thing built to spread power becomes the most efficient tool ever made for concentrating it.

The revolution does not fail. It gets hired.

That last line is the whole pattern compressed. The radical tool keeps working exactly as designed. It just ends up working for the people it was supposed to overthrow, because they were the ones with the resources to deploy it at scale. Liberation and concentration are not opposites here. They are the first and second acts of the same story.

## We have seen this movie

The internet was going to democratize knowledge. In one sense it did. If you already knew what to ask, you got the greatest research library in human history, free, in your pocket. The educated got a superpower.

Everyone else got something else. They got an attention-extraction machine, tuned by the best engineers alive to take more of their day, every day, forever. Same cable, same screen, same company in many cases. One tool, two products, sorted by who you already were when you arrived. The internet did not erase the gap between the informed and the captured. It widened it and made it invisible, because both groups appear to be doing the identical thing. They are both "online."

Social media is the cleanest case, because the mechanism is naked. It was sold as connection. The business model was attention. So the product optimized for whatever holds attention, not for whatever helps you, and the two came apart fast. Nobody in the building chose to harm a generation. The incentive chose it for them. That is the part people miss when they look for villains. Recapture rarely needs a villain. It needs a business model.

Crypto is the case closest to my own history, so I will not flatter it. It promised freedom from state money. Permissionless, borderless, no central authority deciding who gets to hold value. A genuine escape hatch.

Look at what actually shipped. The largest assets in the entire industry are not some new sovereign currency. They are dollars. Dollar stablecoins now make up the overwhelming majority of stablecoin supply, and stablecoins themselves have become the asset the whole space actually uses to move money. Roughly ninety-nine percent of that supply is denominated in dollars. The two biggest issuers together hold well over a hundred billion in US Treasuries, which quietly makes them one of the largest holders of American government debt on Earth, ahead of entire wealthy nations. The 2025 US stablecoin law then made this official, requiring issuers to back their tokens with cash and short-term Treasuries, hardwiring dollar demand into the rails of the rebellion.

So the technology built to free people from state money became the most efficient instrument for extending state money ever invented. We did not escape the dollar. We gave it a faster horse and a global distribution network it never had before. The revolution did not fail. It got hired by the very currency it was supposed to replace.

## Why AI is the dangerous one

AI runs the same play, deeper, because the resource being concentrated this time is not money or attention. It is intelligence itself.

The bottom of the stack becomes a commodity. Cheap models, open weights, good-enough intelligence available to everyone for almost nothing. That part is real, and it is genuinely good, and I do not want to lose sight of it. More people have access to more capability than at any point in history.

But the frontier, the part that actually compounds, stays in very few hands. Training a frontier model now requires compute that only a handful of companies on the planet can assemble, and that handful is pulling away from everyone else, not converging with them. The top labs are growing their compute faster than the world is growing its compute. They share less about how their systems work, not more. The gap between the best available intelligence and the commodity intelligence is widening, and it can run thousands-fold in capability while staying completely invisible, because everyone involved can say, truthfully, that they are "using AI."

That is the recapture pattern at the level of thought. A few people own the machine that will increasingly price, plan, and decide everything, and the rest of us rent a weaker version of our own capabilities back from them. The risk was never that the machine turns evil. The risk is that we run the ad model one more time, this time not on attention but on cognition, and wake up in a world where the gap between the augmented and the merely served is the deepest inequality there has ever been.

## Longevity is the next one

I will name one more, because it is coming and almost no one is watching for it. Longevity.

The science is becoming real. We are starting to treat aging as something we can slow, and eventually as something we can reverse, rather than a fixed fact. This is the most important frontier there is, and I want it more than almost anything.

Watch the recapture form anyway. The first decade of any breakthrough is expensive, scarce, and gated by wealth. If :trail[extended healthy life]{slug=the-longevity-imperative} arrives the way every prior technology arrived, the rich do not just get more money than the poor. They get more years of life. Time itself becomes the asset that concentrates. That is :trail[the two-tier world]{slug=the-two-tier-future} taken to its absolute limit, a split not in what you can afford but in how long you exist. I am a longevity optimist precisely because I refuse to let it land that way by default.

## I am still optimistic

None of this is an argument against the technology. I am about as optimistic as a person can be, and I want all of it, faster. The upside is civilizational. We end diseases, we leave the planet, we lift the floor under billions of lives. That future is real and it is worth sprinting toward.

But optimism without vigilance is just marketing. The technology is never the question. The implementation is the whole question. The same tool that concentrates power can be built to distribute it. That is not a law of physics deciding the outcome. That is a choice, made by builders, about which side of the pattern they want to work on.

So here is where I stand. I am a techno-optimist and a power-skeptic, and I do not think those two things are in tension. They are the same conviction seen from two sides. I believe these tools are the best thing that has ever happened to us, and I believe, on the evidence of every prior cycle, that they will be captured unless someone builds the layer that resists capture.

I would rather build that layer than narrate its absence. The recapture is not destiny. It is just the default. And defaults are the easiest thing in the world to change, if you decide early enough to be the one who changes them.

Optimist about the technology. Skeptic about who ends up holding it. Builder on the side that resists.
