People treat optimism as a personality. The cheerful ones have it, the gloomy ones do not, and that is the end of the conversation. This is wrong, and the mistake costs us the future.

Optimism is a direction you point your most ambitious people.

That is the whole thing. A civilization gets the future it aims its best talent and its best capital at. Point a generation of brilliant builders at selling ads more efficiently and you get exactly the century we are drifting toward, more captured, more distracted, more clever about extracting a little more from the same human appetites. Point the same people at disease, at energy, at leaving the planet, and you get a different century entirely. Same talent. Different destination. The mood had nothing to do with it.

"Things will work out" is a way of opting out. Real optimism is specific and demanding. It names where the most ambitious effort should go and then argues for sending it there, against the gravity of whatever is most profitable right now.

The test of an optimist is what they would point the next thousand brilliant people at, and whether they will say it out loud. The cheerful drift is the easy part. The aim is the work.

I would point them up. At the frontiers whose payoff has no ceiling. That is the bet, and it is a choice.
